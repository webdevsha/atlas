01 / The Context

The "Science Project" Trap

Most researchers fail to transition from "Novelty" to "Utility".

Investors do not fund science experiments. They fund de-risked venture assets that have a path to monopoly.

Science Project (The Lab)

  • Goal: Maximize Technical Novelty
  • Funding: Grants (Endless Cycle)
  • Risk: "Will it work?" (Technical Risk)
  • Outcome: A Paper / Thesis

Venture Asset (The Goal)

  • Goal: Maximize Sovereign Utility
  • Funding: Contracts & Equity
  • Risk: "Will they buy?" (Market/Reg Risk)
  • Outcome: A Commercial Monopoly
01 / The Definition

Deeptech != High-Tech Software

Deeptech refers to startups whose business model is built on tangible engineering innovation or scientific discovery.

The BCG Definition

"Deep tech companies are built on tangible scientific discoveries or engineering innovations. They solve big problems and have no existing market solution."

— Boston Consulting Group (BCG)

01 / The Malaysian Gap

The "Valley of Death" in Malaysia

High Output, Low Outcome

Malaysia ranks high in scientific publications (22nd globally in 2020), but commercialization rates from universities remain below 5%.

Source: Academy of Sciences Malaysia (ASM)

The Funding Gap

Government grants (Cradle/MOSTI) cover up to TRL 4-5. VCs demand TRL 8-9 (Revenue). There is almost zero capital for TRL 6-7 (Pilot/Industrialization).

Source: 12th Malaysia Plan Analysis

Regulatory Lag

Policies often lag innovation by 3-5 years (e.g., Drone laws, AI frameworks), forcing startups to move abroad to test.

Source: Khazanah Research Institute

The Reality Check: If you build a Deeptech startup in Malaysia using a SaaS playbook, you will run out of money at TRL 5. You need a "Sovereign Utility" strategy to bridge the gap.

02 / Historical Context

Deeptech is Born from Sovereign Need

NASA Apollo (1960s)

The microchip industry wasn't built by consumer demand. It was built because the US Air Force needed guidance computers for Minuteman II missiles that were light enough to fly.

The Lesson

Deeptech starts by solving a National Security or Infrastructure problem, not by selling gadgets to consumers.

Silicon Valley (Fairchild)

Fairchild Semiconductor's first major customers weren't radio makers. They were defense contractors. The "Venture Capital" model emerged to de-risk these technologies for the state.

The Lesson

Align your startup with a "Sovereign Mandate" (Food, Energy, Defense) to unlock the biggest, earliest checks.

03 / Framework

The Moat Radar

Investors ask "What's your moat?". "We have a patent" is the wrong answer. You need a structural defense.

A

Technical Moat

Hard to replicate physics or engineering. Requires years of R&D.

Example: Proprietary Enzyme Synthesis

B

Regulatory Moat

Compliance is so hard, nobody else wants to do it.

Example: FDA Class III Approval

C

Economic Moat

Unit economics that only work at your scale or efficiency.

Example: Cost per kg to Orbit

Case Study

Aphelia Space

Tech: Wireless Power. Regulatory Moat: Operating in lunar shadowed regions requires compliance standards that Aphelia helped write. This locks out competitors who don't know the "Law of the Moon".

05 / Strategy

Engineering for the Check

The Mistake: Building "features" because you can.
The Fix: Building "evidence" that unlocks the next tranche of money.

Mission Objectives (Ends)

The binary milestones an investor needs to see to believe the risk is reduced.

  • "It can fly for 10 minutes."
  • "It costs less than $100/kg."

Mission Requirements (Means)

The specific engineering specs needed to hit the objective. Nothing more.

  • Battery density: 400 Wh/kg
  • Airframe weight: < 50kg
