Strategic Positioning

Why Strategy First?

Deeptech is expensive. Building the wrong research direction leads to the loss of years and millions.

Often, this first step reveals your "Kryptonite" and forces a necessary PIVOT before you spend a single Ringgit on hardware.

Cautionary Tale

A biotech startup developed a sustainable animal feed. Scientifically sound, but they failed to position against existing supply chain logistics. Currently filing for bankruptcy.
Lesson: Problem Fit > Science Fit.

The Power of Narrative

Silicon Valley & YCombinator emphasize Storytelling. For radical ideas, you aren't just selling a product; you are selling a future state of the world.

The Master Tool Slide 11 / 18

How to choose the Subsystem?

"The Monkey and The Pedestal"

Astro Teller (Captain of Moonshots at Google X):

"If you're trying to get a monkey to recite Shakespeare on a pedestal, don't start by building the pedestal. Building the pedestal is easy. All the risk is in training the monkey."

Your Core Deeptech MVP must be the Monkey (The Hardest Tech Risk), not the Pedestal (The Easy Stuff).

Identify your Technical Moat.
That is your Monkey.

Defining the Build Slide 16 / 18

MVP Development Strategy

Step 1: Simulation is the MVP

Before bending metal, bend bits. Deeptech investors accept high-fidelity simulations as proof of concept.

Case Study: Hermeus

Hypersonic jet startup accepted into YCombinator. Their "MVP" was not a plane, but a verified computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulation of the engine intake.

Step 2: The RM 100k Challenge

Can we build the Core Tech Subsystem (The Monkey) using only Cradle CIP Spark (RM 100k) in 1.5 years?

  • Yes, if you focus ONLY on the Subsystem.
  • No, if you try to build the whole Moonshot.
